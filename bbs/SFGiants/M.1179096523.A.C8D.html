標題[新聞] Lewis hits for the cycle
時間Mon May 14 06:48:42 2007
Lewis gets two singles, double, triple, homer vs. Rockies. DENVER -- Upon Fred Lewis' recall from Triple-A Fresno last Thursday, San Francisco Giants manager Bruce Bochy discussed his eagerness to see the 26-year-old outfielder develop."I think he has a chance to be a special player," Bochy said. "He has all the tools." Lewis developed quickly on Sunday. Playing only his 16th Major League game and starting just his fourth game with the Giants, the left-handed-batting Lewis hit for the cycle while helping the Giants roar to a 15-1 lead over the Colorado Rockies. Batting leadoff and playing center field, Lewis doubled in the first inning and lofted a three-run homer in the fourth off Colorado starter Taylor Buchholz. It was Lewis' first Major League home run. Defying the percentages, Lewis drove an RBI triple to left-center field in the fifth inning off Rockies left-hander Tom Martin. Lewis finished his cycle in the seventh inning when he led off with a line-drive single to right on a 1-2 pitch from Denny Bautista. The Coors Field crowd, obviously paying attention, gave Lewis a warm ovation after his cycle became official. He became the first Giants rookie to reach this accomplishment since Dave Kingman did so on April 16, 1972, at Houston. Lewis became the 22nd player in franchise history to hit for the cycle, a list that includes Hall of Famers Mel Ott, Fred Lindstrom and Bill Terry. Lewis is also the ninth San Francisco Giant to achieve the feat and the first to do so since Randy Winn on Aug. 15, 2005, at Cincinnati. For good measure, Lewis singled again with two outs in the seventh off Denny Bautista toward the end of a six-run inning.
