標題Giants' Morris too crafty for Dodgers
時間Wed Apr 25 13:37:28 2007
04/25/2007 12:56 AM ET Giants' Morris too crafty for Dodgers Right-hander pitches into eighth inning to earn victory By Chris Haft / MLB.com LOS ANGELES -- Lengthening San Francisco's Major League-high winning streak to six games Tuesday night, the Giants outlasted the Los Angeles Dodgers, 5-3. The decision also ended the Dodgers' eight-game winning streak against San Francisco. It was the fourth-longest winning streak for either team in the historic rivalry. Matt Morris (3-0) cruised through seven innings, allowing one run and three hits, before yielding a pair of seventh-inning runs. Jack Taschner escaped a jam by forcing an inning-ending double-play grounder from Luis Gonzalez. Armando Benitez allowed a pair of ninth-inning singles but emerged with his fifth save in as many opportunities. Dave Roberts singled and scored a first-inning run to open the Giants' scoring and added a fifth-inning homer, his first round-tripper in 389 at-bats dating from last May 26. Randy Winn went 2-for-4 and drove in two runs. Chris Haft is a reporter for MLB.com.
