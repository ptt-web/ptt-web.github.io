Baldelli's knee fine, should be available Tuesday Rocco Baldelli ran onto the field today wearing a pad on his badly bruised left knee, but that seemed to be the only evidence of his bone-jarring collision with the right field stands Saturday. Baldelli said that he woke up feeling sore but generally fine, and reported no problems working out today. He should be available when the Sox take on Detroit Tuesday. Baldelli started in right field Saturday, and hit a two-run home run to left. But in the fifth inning, he slid hard into the wall trying to chase down a foul ball, and had to be removed from the game. Kotsay should return Tuesday Five months after undergoing back surgery, and a month after injuring his calf during his back rehab, Mark Kotsay should finally return to the active roster Tuesday. Kotsay began his second rehab stint with Pawtucket last week, and has traveled with the PawSox on their latest road trip to Louisville and Indianapolis. He has gone 10-for-33 through his 10 rehab games in Pawtucket. Jeff Bailey would likely return to Pawtucket when Kotsay comes back. John Smoltz pitched five innings today with Low-A Greenville against Charleston. He allowed one run on four hits, walking none and striking out six. He threw 73 pitches, 52 of them strikes. The outing was Smoltz's most aggressive rehab start yet.
