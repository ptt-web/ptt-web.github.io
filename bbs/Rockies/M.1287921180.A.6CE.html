Tulo may go Melo's way too 十月 20 Denver Post columnist Mark Kiszla http://tinyurl.com/2bdwu5s "With Carmelo Anthony's existence in a Nuggets uniform listed as day to day, the local NBA team could soon be in the market for a scorer and a gate attraction. Since Troy Tulowitzki was sitting behind the Denver bench during an exhibition game on Tuesday night, I figured it couldn't hurt to ask. Have you got game, Tulo? Would there any interest in moonlighting as a shooting guard for the Nuggets between now and the start of spring training? "I'm a half-court shot specialist," replied Tulowitzki, a sly grin creasing his face. Tulo apparently has the same unlimited range on a basketball court that he exhibits playing shortstop for the Rockies. A shooter without a conscience? Hmm, that's interesting. I'd pay to watch Tulo launch 25-foot jumpers. But don't the Nuggets already have that position filled? Think his name is J.R. Smith. If the Rockies are paying Tulo $5.5 million for the upcoming baseball season, however, how much could it cost the Nuggets to sign him for 35 games? "Tulo told us he's available," joked Nuggets front-office executive Masai Ujiri. "And he said he'd be willing to sign for the NBA minimum." As a senior in high school, Tulowitzki averaged 22.6 points per game. "Hey," Anthony said, "22 a game anywhere is good." OK, OK. Despite the fact Tulowitzki earned all-state recognition as a prep basketball star in California, don't get your hopes up. He isn't signing with the Nuggets or ever playing a game in the NBA. Enjoy Tulo and Melo while you can. Because, in all likelihood, they won't play in Colorado from the beginning to the end of their prospective Hall of Fame careers. The era when John Elway and Joe Sakic were neighbors you could count on? Those days are gone forever. "I love him," Anthony said of Tulowitzki, "just for what he's able to bring to the game and watch him get better every year." Tulowitzki attended the Nuggets' preseason game against Oklahoma City, knowing full well that Anthony could be gone in a trade tomorrow or next month. The New York Knicks are working diligently to put together a package that would entice Denver to get serious in talking a deal for Melo. "He's one of the best players in the game," said Tulo- witzki, who retired to the Blue Sky Grill in the arena at halftime so he could watch the New York Yankees in the American League Championship Series on one television screen and track Anthony on another monitor. Tulowitzki's take on Melo leaving the Nuggets in a will- he-stay-or-go limbo is different, depending on whether the shortstop examines the situation with his heart or brain. "As a fan, I definitely want him to say in Denver," Tulo- witzki said. "But, sometimes, business is business." Business can be cruel to a fan's heart. Just because the Nuggets made it to the Western Conference finals in 2008, there's no guarantee they will ever go that deep into the playoffs again. That's why it's the responsibility of the Rockies to surround Tulowitzki with as much talent as the team can afford before the 26-year-old shortstop, outfielder Carlos Gonzalez and pitcher Ubaldo Jimenez can start exploring their free-agent options. With roots in Brooklyn, Anthony knows what the real No. 1 sport is in New York. "Baseball was my first sport," he said.
