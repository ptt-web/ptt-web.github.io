標題[新聞] Winter Meetings: Day 2
時間Wed Dec 10 08:53:27 2008
Greetings from the workroom at the Bellagio, where things are about ready to get underway for the second day of the Winter Meetings. As far as the Yankees are concerned, they could use a little less conversation and a little more action. CC Sabathia has left town and nobody seems very optimistic that he will sign anywhere until after this week. A.J. Burnett would be "crazy," one source said, to put his name on a contract before Sabathia helps to set the market for a top-flight starting pitcher. Derek Lowe might sign, but then you remember he's represented by Scott Boras and that seems less likely than the previous two. So if Brian Cashman wants to add a name to the Yankees' roster before the flight home on Thursday, maybe it makes sense with what the New York Daily News is reporting: a two-year pact in the $30 million ballpark for Ben Sheets -- some risk, sure, but a reasonable enough deal that it wouldn't preclude the Yankees from signing Sabathia or Burnett. Or hey, even Mark Teixeira, though Cashman was firm in saying the Yankees have not made an offer to the first baseman. Pitching is the priority, as they've banged the drum time and time again. As a side note, there are so many wild rumors flying around out there that you almost have to take it as pure entertainment.
