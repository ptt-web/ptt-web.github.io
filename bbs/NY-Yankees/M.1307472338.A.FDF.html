http://ppt.cc/~Qh5 One way or the other, Matt Duran's baseball future is going to hinge on playing in The Bronx. Duran, a New Rochelle High School player who is committed to Fordham, was drafted by the Yankees with their fourth round pick Tuesday afternoon. Listed by the Yankees as a third baseman, Duran was the 23rd best prospect in New York, according to Baseball America, and outside of its top 200. The site doesn't have a profile up of Duran, who was a member of Hank's Yanks, a team sponsored by Yankees co-owner Hank Steinbrenner. Fordham University does on its website, though, and here it is: Duran, a 6-1 infielder from New Rochelle, New York, is a four-year varsity baseball player at New Rochelle High School. He is a two-time All-League selection, and was named to the All-Section team as a junior, when he also garnered team MVP honors. He was rated by Perfect Game USA as a top national prospect, and was selected Second Team All-Star for Lo-Hud baseball by The Journal News. In addition to playing for New Rochelle, Duran plays for Hank's Yanks, a team sponsored by Hank Steinbrenner, General Partner of the New York Yankees, and the New York Yankees area code team, for which he hit .300 during the Area Code Games in California, which has grown into one of the highest regarded high school talent tournaments in the country.
