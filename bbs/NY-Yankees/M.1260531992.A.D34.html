Source: New York Yankees could turn to Hideki Matsui, minors to fill DH slot if Johnny Damon balks BY Mark Feinsand DAILY NEWS SPORTS WRITER INDIANAPOLIS - Brian Cashman left the winter meetings Thursday having filled two big holes on his 2010 roster, yet there is still work left for him to do before the Yankees report to Tampa 10 weeks from now. With Andy Pettitte back for 2010, the pressure to bring in another starter from outside the organization isn't as strong. And the Bombers added an All-Star center fielder in Curtis Granderson. The Yankees do, however, need to address their designated hitter spot. Johnny Damon remains the club's top choice to fill the slot, since he could play in the outfield or be the DH, giving Joe Girardi flexibility - especially if he wants to rest Granderson against tough lefthanders. But Damon and his agent, Scott Boras, seem intent on getting a three- or four-year deal, something the Yankees are not interested in. Cashman and Boras will continue to negotiate, but if Damon is unwilling to take a two-year contract in the next two weeks or so, a source said the Yankees would likely turn their attention to Hideki Matsui, who is willing to accept a one-year deal to return to the Bronx. The Yankees could also pursue either Mark DeRosa or Mike Cameron if they don't bring back Damon, the source said. 大門跟Boras堅持3年以上合約，持續與現金人協商中。 松井願意接受1年合約。 Cashman speculated that Scranton first baseman Juan Miranda could fill a part-time DH role next season, although it sounded an awful lot like the GM's words four years ago when he insisted that Bubba Crosby would be the team's center fielder. He then went on to sign Damon to a four-year, $52 million pact. 現金人慣用手法。 "We have Jorge Posada, Mark Teixeira, A-Rod; guys who can swing over (to DH) on their days of rest," Cashman said. "We have people we can play in that position internally right now. I'm not trying to oversell anybody, but Juan Miranda will do a nice job as a DH if that's what we have to do. Is that the right way to go? I think the market will dictate that." That's Cashman's way of telling Damon and Boras that he won't be forced into anything this winter. With roughly $180 million already committed for 2010, Cashman is looking to to keep the payroll below $200 million, but he says he's open to how that money gets spent. "If I see an attractive starter that makes sense within our criteria or an attractive position player, I'll gravitate to it," Cashman said. "I've got 'X' amount of dollars to spend. I'm open to an array of choices right now." Cashman remains engaged in trade discussions with several teams, including the Blue Jays, who continue to shop Roy Halladay. The Angels and Phillies are believed to be the two front-runners, and while Cashman admits to being cautious about trading young players such as Joba Chamberlain, Phil Hughes and Jesus Montero, he said nobody is untouchable. 現金人持續幫鳥王抬價。 "I'm hesitant to move certain young players because of what they can do for us in the here and now or in the future and the salary off-set they provide," he said. "At the same time, for the right player, I'll move anybody." With Rich Harden and Randy Wolf no longer on the market, the available starting pitching is now limited to John Lackey, Ben Sheets, Jason Marquis, Justin Duchscherer, Joel Pineiro and Jon Garland. The Yankees are preparing Chamberlain and Hughes as starters, although one could be moved to the bullpen if Cashman brought in another starter via free agency or trade. "I'm definitely not in a position right now where I feel like I'm ready to do anything," Cashman said. "The next step isn't ready to happen now.
