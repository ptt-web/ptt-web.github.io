Pettitte 感覺比較好了，但應該會跳過下次先發，可能由Mitre代替出征。 NYY的四核心最近連續出現小問題，一些記者開玩笑把 The Core Four 改成 The Sore Four. Posada可能還要坐板凳， 今天會知道要不要進DL。不過Mo週末確定可以面對紅襪。 球迷和媒體又在炒作週末的世仇對抗氣氛，鐵爺說 其實和以前的每一場基襪比賽沒什麼不一樣，努力贏球就是了。 AJ則熱血沸騰：俺可是為此而活的！ http://news.bostonherald.com/sports/baseball/other_mlb/view/20100506 andy_pettitte_likely_to_miss_next_start/srvc=home&position=recent Andy Pettitte likely to miss next start By Erik Boland / Newsday Thursday, May 6, 2010 BOSTON - Speaking shortly after Wednesday’s game that saw Andy Pettitte leave with what was later diagnosed as mild inflammation of his left elbow, Brian Cashman said the lefty was likely to miss his next start Tuesday in Detroit. The Yankees general manager said Thursday that was still his thought, though he said a final decision will come Friday. "He feels better," Cashman said of Pettitte. "That’s my inclination (that he ’ll be skipped), but we’ll talk tomorrow to our trainers, the pitching coach, the manager, everyone, and then we’ll make a decision as a group." Cashman said if Pettitte doesn’t start Tuesday, Sergio Mitre likely will, ahead of Alfredo Aceves, who has also been discussed. "I think he’s more stretched out," Cashman said of Mitre. Pettitte isn’t the only Yankee banged up as they begin a three-game series at Fenway Park [map] against the Red Sox [team stats]. Catcher Jorge Posada left Monday’s game with pain in his right calf and though he and manager Joe Girardi have said he’s improved daily since then, the catcher is questionable for the weekend series and a trip to the DL will depend on how he feels today. The best news probably revolves around Mariano Rivera, who has not pitched since last Friday because of a stiff left side. After Wednesday’s game Girardi said he was "pretty positive" he’d have the closer this weekend. While headline writers have had fun in recent days exchanging "sore" for "core" in describing the Yankees’ esteemed quartet of veterans, it’s served as a reminder of how fortunate the Yankees were last year in regard to injuries. "That’s usually part of a championship run is staying healthy," Cashman said. "It goes part and parcel." And the Yankees, to this point of the season, still have had the fewest number of players (two) to visit the disabled list: Curtis Granderson and Chan Ho Park. The Yankees, though banged up, are coming off a sweep of the Orioles and have won seven of their last eight. They take on the suddenly surging Red Sox who, after an almost unfathomable sweep at the hands of the Orioles last weekend, took the first three games of their four-game series against the Angels this week to climb back to .500. "We answer this question every time we play the Red Sox; it’s great for the fans. The fans love it, it’s an exciting series," Mark Teixeira said. "They’ re going to be into it, everyone’s going to be watching. But at the same time, the day that we played (Wednesday) counts the same in the standings as the first one we play against Boston. We just have to go out there and try to win the game and not worry about what else is going on." A.J. Burnett, Sunday night’s starter, isn’t quite as detached when it comes to the rivalry. "I live for it," Burnett said. "I think it always brings the warrior out in you; the best in you. You go into a packed house as a visitor, the underdog, with your back against the wall. And it shows you what you’re really about." (Newsday’s Kimberley A.
