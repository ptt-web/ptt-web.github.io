標題[外電] Dodgers place Loaiza on DL
時間Thu May 8 09:59:25 2008
LOS ANGELES -- Esteban Loaiza, taken out of the rotation after a 2 1/3-inning start on Saturday and with no apparent role on the staff, was placed on the 15-day disabled list after Wednesday's game with tightness and spasming in the right shoulder blade. The Dodgers, who are off on Thursday, will replace Loaiza before Friday night's game with Houston. It most likely will be a pitcher and the leading candidate to be promoted is Triple-A right-hander Yhency Brazoban, who would be returning to the Major Leagues after missing most of last season with shoulder surgery. Loaiza has a 1-2 record and a 5.62 ERA, having been given the ball three times as the fifth starter, most recently when he allowed five runs in 2 1/3 innings at Coors Field. Because of off-days this month, the Dodgers won't need a fifth starter until May 17. Loaiza, disabled retroactively to Sunday, won't be active at that point. Jason Schmidt, who could take over the fifth-starter job at some point, is nearing the start of an injury rehabilitation assignment, nearly 11 months after right shoulder surgery, but he won't be ready by May 17. Another option would be 20-year-old phenom Clayton Kershaw, who has a 1.08 ERA at Double-A Jacksonville and who threw one inning in relief on Wednesday.
